# Possessive pronouns

Possessives agree with the gender and number of the **possessed** noun (not the possessor). Third-person forms (**hans**, **hennes**, **dess**, **deras**) are invariable.

| Possessor | Utrum (en) | Neutrum (ett) | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|
| **jag** | **min** | **mitt** | **mina** |
| **du** | **din** | **ditt** | **dina** |
| **han** | **hans** | **hans** | **hans** |
| **hon** | **hennes** | **hennes** | **hennes** |
| **den / det** | **dess** | **dess** | **dess** |
| **vi** | **vår** | **vårt** | **våra** |
| **ni** | **er** | **ert** | **era** |
| **de** | **deras** | **deras** | **deras** |

## Reflexive possessive

Third-person possessors use **sin**/**sitt**/**sina** when the possessed noun belongs to the subject of the clause.

| Possessor | Utrum (en) | Neutrum (ett) | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|
| **han / hon / den / det** | **sin** | **sitt** | **sina** |

## Examples

- **Han kör sin bil.** — *He drives his own car.*
- **Han kör hennes bil.** — *He drives her car.*
- **Han kör hans bil.** — *He drives some other man's car.*
